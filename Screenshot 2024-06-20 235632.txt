The chart shows the growth of the active client base of the National Stock Exchange of India (NSE) in millions (mn) from fiscal year (FY) 2014 to FY 2023. The y-axis represents the number of active clients in millions and the x-axis represents fiscal year. The chart title is  “Rapidly growing NSE active client base (mn)”.

There is a clear upward trend in the number of active NSE clients over the period FY 2014 to FY 2023. The number of active clients has grown from 0.14 million in FY 2014 to 40.8 million in FY 2023. This represents a growth of over 29,000% over the ten year period.

There are some fluctuations in the growth rate over the years. For example, the number of active clients grew by 1.58 million between FY 2021 and FY 2022 and then by 4.28 million between FY 2022 and FY 2023. This suggests that the growth of the NSE’s active client base is accelerating.

It is important to note that the data in this chart only shows the number of active clients on the NSE. The total number of demat accounts in India is much higher. However, the data in this chart does suggest that there is a growing interest in stock market investing in India. This could be due to a number of factors, including rising incomes, increasing financial literacy, and the strong performance of the Indian stock market in recent years.

Overall Summary

The number of active clients on the National Stock Exchange of India (NSE) has grown significantly in recent years. This suggests that there is a growing interest in stock market investing in India. This could be due to a number of factors, including rising incomes, increasing financial literacy, and the strong performance of the Indian stock market in recent years.
