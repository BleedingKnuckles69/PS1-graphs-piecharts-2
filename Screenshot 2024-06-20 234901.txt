The pie chart titled "Mutual Funds" shows the percentage of investment held by different investor categories in India.

    Resident Individuals account for the largest portion of mutual fund investors, at approximately 60%.
    Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) constitute roughly 20% of investors.
    The remaining 20% is divided among various investor categories including Bodies Corporate, Qualified Institutional Buyers (QIBs), Promotional and Employee groups, and others.

Overall, resident individuals are the primary investors in mutual funds in India, followed by NRIs. Institutional investors like QIBs and Bodies Corporate make up a smaller proportion of mutual fund investors in India.

Important to Note: It is important to acknowledge that this pie chart does not depict the specific types of mutual fund investments tercih [preferred] by each investor category.
